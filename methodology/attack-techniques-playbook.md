# Attack Techniques Playbook

This document describes common techniques used in digital fraud, phishing, and impersonation operations.

---

## Social Engineering
Attackers manipulate users through urgency, fear, or trust exploitation.

Common examples:
- Fake account verification requests
- Urgent payment notifications
- Fake security alerts

---

## Phishing
Fraudulent messages redirect users to credential harvesting pages.

Common techniques:
- Fake login portals
- Email impersonation
- SMS phishing campaigns

---

## Brand Impersonation
Attackers mimic legitimate brands to gain user trust.

Examples:
- Fake customer support channels
- Promotional scam websites
- Fake brand social media profiles

---

## Fake E-commerce Operations
Fraudsters deploy fake stores to collect payments.

Indicators:
- Unrealistic discounts
- Limited payment methods
- No delivery fulfillment

---

## Marketplace Scams
Fraud occurs outside legitimate platform protections.

Examples:
- Payment requested outside platform
- Fake shipping confirmations

---

## Analyst Notes
Attack techniques evolve constantly; monitoring new patterns is essential.
