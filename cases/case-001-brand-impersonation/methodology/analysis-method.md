# Digital Risk Analysis Methodology

## Objective
This methodology describes the general process used to analyze potential digital risk cases, including phishing, fraud, and brand impersonation scenarios.

## Analysis Steps

### 1. Initial Identification
- Analyze the reported URL or platform content
- Verify if the content mimics legitimate brands or services
- Check for suspicious domain patterns or misleading information

### 2. Infrastructure Observation
- Identify hosting patterns or suspicious infrastructure indicators
- Evaluate domain structure and possible abuse patterns

### 3. Risk Classification
Risk is evaluated based on:
- Potential financial damage
- Credential theft possibility
- Brand abuse impact
- User data exposure

### 4. Impact Evaluation
Determine potential consequences for:
- Users
- Companies
- Platforms

### 5. Recommended Actions
Possible actions include:
- Reporting to hosting providers
- Reporting to domain registrars
- Platform abuse notification
- Monitoring recurrence patterns

## Continuous Improvement
Analysis techniques evolve as new abuse methods and fraud patterns emerge.
